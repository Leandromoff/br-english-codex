A/an, plurals: singular and plural forms

Use a before consonant sounds and an before vowel sounds. Most nouns form the plural by adding -s (book → books). Nouns ending in -s, -x, -sh, -ch take -es (bus → buses). For words ending in a consonant + -y, change y to i and add -es (city → cities). Irregular plurals include man → men, woman → women, child → children, person → people.

  • I have a pen. → I have two pens.
  • She saw an apple. → She bought three apples.
  • There is a bus at 7 a.m. → There are many buses.
  • My city is big. → These cities are big.
  • One child is playing. → Two children are playing.
