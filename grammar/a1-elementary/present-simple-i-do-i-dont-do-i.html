Present simple: I do, I don’t, Do I?

Visão Geral

In present simple verbs, we use do/does in questions and don't/doesn't in negatives.

Form

Affirmative Negative Interrogative
I/you/we/they work I/you/we/they don't work Do I/you/we/they work?
he/she/it works he/she/it doesn't work Does he/she/it work?

Spelling of he/she/it

The form is the same for all the other persons except he/she/it. The spelling for he/she/it is different.

Verb He/She/It
work works
study studies
go goes
fix fixes
have has

Present simple use

Habits or actions that happen regularly

I wash my hair every day.

I never go to the library.

I go to the library on Saturdays.

Permanent situations or things that are always or often true

I don’t drink coffee.

She’s very tall.

I have two brothers.

Water boils at 100 degrees.

I like soup.
